# LIQWD — Seize Your Story

A working framework for building the LIQWD pitch, based on Michael Skok's
**"Seize Your Story"** (Startup Secrets). Use it for investor pitches, but also
for recruiting, sales calls, and rallying partners — the same story should hold
up everywhere.

> **How to use this doc.** Every section has two parts: a **Starter draft**
> pulled from LIQWD's approved brand copy (a first pass, not gospel — rewrite
> it), and **Your turn** prompts to work through. Fill in the blanks marked
> `▸`. Do the ABC part first (it's how you design the story); deliver it in the
> STORY order.

---

## The two frameworks, in one breath

Skok teaches two halves of the same skill:

| | Framework | Direction | Purpose |
|---|-----------|-----------|---------|
| **Design** | **ABC** | Backwards — start from the outcome you want | Figure out *what* to say |
| **Deliver** | **STORY** | Forwards — the order you actually tell it | Figure out *how* to say it |

**Design backwards (ABC):** decide the **A**ct you want, prove why they should
**B**elieve, and anchor it in what they **C**are about.

**Deliver forwards (STORY):** **S**et the stage → build **T**ension → reveal the
**O**pportunity → show the **R**esolution → land the **Y**es.

The mistake to avoid (the whole point of the video): **don't open with
buzzwords.** "An AI-powered platform that leverages machine learning to
optimize broker workflows" makes people tune out. Open with a real person, a
real friction, a real moment.

# Part 1 — Design Backwards (ABC)

Work these in the order **C → B → A**. You design from what the audience cares
about, back to the ask.

## C — Care (what does the audience actually care about?)

Before anything, know who's in the room and what keeps them up at night. The
story is about *them*, not about LIQWD.

**Starter draft — audiences LIQWD needs to move:**
- **Realtors / agents (users):** wasting time bouncing between disconnected
  broker portals, scattered files, and outdated project updates; missing
  commission / incentive opportunities because the info is buried.
- **Brokerages / builders (supply side):** getting their new-home inventory in
  front of the right verified agents, cleanly.
- **Investors:** a defensible wedge into Ontario new-home real estate, a
  verified-professional network with a real moat, and a path to revenue.

**Your turn:**
- ▸ Who is *this specific* telling for? (one audience — pick it)
- ▸ What do they care about *before* they've ever heard of LIQWD?
- ▸ What's the one thing that, if you nailed it, makes them lean in?

## B — Believe (why should they believe this will work?)

This is where you make the case *and* disarm the obvious risks. Belief =
evidence + credibility − unanswered doubts.

**Starter draft — LIQWD's belief points:**
- **Trust moat:** access is gated by **RECO verification** — a verified
  broker-to-broker network the general public can't enter. Hard to fake, hard
  to copy.
- **Real workflow, not a directory:** one organized portal replaces many —
  browse active projects, submit new properties, surface commission / pricing /
  incentive opportunities.
- **Focus:** built specifically for **Ontario** new homes, not a generic
  everything-everywhere MLS clone.
- **Built and shipping:** core product is live — auth, verification gating,
  project browsing, lead capture, submissions, admin review console.

**Risks to address head-on (name them before they do):**
- ▸ Why won't builders / brokerages just keep using their own portals?
- ▸ What's the wedge that gets the first 100 verified agents on?
- ▸ How does "free for realtors" become a business? (who pays, and when?)
- ▸ What stops a well-funded incumbent from copying it?

**Your turn:**
- ▸ Your three strongest proof points (traction, insight, or unfair advantage):
- ▸ The single biggest objection you'll get — and your one-line answer:

## A — Act (what's the one thing you want them to do?)

End every telling with a specific, singular ask. Vague asks get vague answers.

**Starter draft (by audience):**
- Investor → ▸ "We're raising `$___` to `___`. Here's what it unlocks."
- Realtor → "Sign up free and get verified" (the product's real CTA)
- Builder / brokerage → ▸ "List your active projects to reach verified agents."

**Your turn:**
- ▸ For your chosen audience, the *one* action you want:
- ▸ Why now? (what makes this the moment to act, not later?)

# Part 2 — Deliver Forwards (STORY)

Now tell it in this order. Keep it human. A named person beats an abstraction
every time.

## S — Set the stage (context)

Drop the audience into a concrete world. A specific agent, a specific Tuesday,
a specific deal on the line.

**Starter draft:**
> Meet an Ontario realtor with a serious buyer for a new-build. To do their job
> they're logging into a handful of separate broker portals, digging through
> scattered PDFs, and working off updates that may already be stale.

**Your turn:**
- ▸ Who is the person at the center of the story? (name them)
- ▸ What are they trying to get done, in one sentence?

## T — Tension (the real problem)

Make the pain felt, not just stated. What does the current mess *cost* them?

**Starter draft:**
> Every hour spent chasing project details across disconnected portals is an
> hour not spent selling. Worse: the opportunities that actually matter —
> better commissions, pricing, incentives — are buried where they're easy to
> miss. The system is working against the professional trying to move fast.

**Your turn:**
- ▸ The cost of the problem in a number or a vivid consequence:
- ▸ Why hasn't this been solved already? (why is it hard / why now?)

## O — Opportunity (the gap)

Zoom out from the one person to the size of the gap. What becomes possible if
this is fixed?

**Starter draft:**
> Ontario has thousands of verified realtors and a constant flow of new-home
> inventory — but no single trusted, broker-only place to work through it.
> Close that gap and you own the workflow where new-home deals actually get
> worked.

**Your turn:**
- ▸ How big is the gap? (market size, # of agents, # of projects, $ of volume —
  get a real number)
- ▸ Why is this a *category*, not a feature?

## R — Resolution (your solution)

Now — and only now — introduce LIQWD. Tie every capability back to the tension
you built.

**Starter draft:**
> **LIQWD** is the broker portal that gives Ontario realtors one place to work
> through new-home inventory. Verified access via RECO. Active projects and
> broker portals in one clean workflow. The commission, pricing, and incentive
> opportunities surfaced instead of buried. Free for verified realtors, built
> in Canada.

**Your turn:**
- ▸ Say what LIQWD is in one plain sentence (no buzzwords — read it aloud):
- ▸ Map each feature to the pain it kills:
  - Feature ▸ ______ → kills the pain of ______
  - Feature ▸ ______ → kills the pain of ______

## Y — You / Yes (the ask + why you)

Two jobs: make the ask (your **A** from ABC), and answer "why *you*?" Investors
back founders, not just markets.

**Your turn:**
- ▸ The ask (restate your Act, out loud, specifically):
- ▸ Why are *you* the team to win this? (unfair insight, background, unfair
  access to this market)
- ▸ The one line you want them repeating after you leave the room:

# Outputs — build these once the sections are drafted

### 1. The one-liner (say it in an elevator, no buzzwords)
> ▸ LIQWD helps `[who]` do `[what]` so they can `[outcome]` — without
> `[the old pain]`.
>
> _Starter:_ LIQWD helps Ontario realtors work through new-home inventory from
> one verified portal — without bouncing between disconnected broker portals
> and stale files.

### 2. The 60-second pitch
Stitch S → T → O → R → Y into ~150 words. Read it aloud and cut anything you'd
never actually say to a person.
> ▸ _(draft here)_

### 3. Pitch-deck outline (STORY → slides)

| # | Slide | Framework beat | LIQWD content |
|---|-------|----------------|---------------|
| 1 | Hook / title | S — set the stage | The realtor's Tuesday |
| 2 | Problem | T — tension | Disconnected portals, buried opportunities |
| 3 | Market / gap | O — opportunity | Ontario new-home volume, # verified agents |
| 4 | Product | R — resolution | The one portal + RECO-verified access |
| 5 | Why it wins | B — believe | Trust moat, focus, live product |
| 6 | Business model | B — believe | ▸ how "free for realtors" pays |
| 7 | Traction | B — believe | ▸ signups, projects listed, usage |
| 8 | Team | Y — you | ▸ why this team |
| 9 | The ask | A — act / Y — yes | ▸ raise + use of funds |

## Checklist before you present

- [ ] Opens with a person and a moment — **not** a buzzword
- [ ] The audience hears their own **Care** in the first 20 seconds
- [ ] Tension is *felt* (a number or a vivid consequence), not just stated
- [ ] LIQWD (Resolution) doesn't appear until after the tension lands
- [ ] Every feature is tied back to a specific pain
- [ ] The biggest objection is answered before they raise it
- [ ] Ends on **one** specific ask — and "why you"
- [ ] You can deliver the whole thing without a single slide
